Problem Formulation

The portfolio optimization problem is formulated as a constrained quadratic optimization with the following mathematical structure:

Decision Variables

$$\mathbf{w} = [w_1, w_2, \ldots, w_n]^T \in \mathbb{R}^n \text{ (portfolio weights vector)}$$ $$n = \text{number of assets/strategies}$$

Objective Function

Maximize Sharpe Ratio:

$$\max S(\mathbf{w}) = \frac{\boldsymbol{\mu}^T\mathbf{w}}{\sqrt{\mathbf{w}^T\boldsymbol{\Sigma}\mathbf{w}}}$$

Since optimizers typically minimize, we solve:

$$\min f(\mathbf{w}) = -\frac{\boldsymbol{\mu}^T\mathbf{w}}{\sqrt{\mathbf{w}^T\boldsymbol{\Sigma}\mathbf{w}}}$$

Complete Mathematical Program

$$\begin{align} \text{minimize} \quad & f(\mathbf{w}) = -\frac{\boldsymbol{\mu}^T\mathbf{w}}{\sqrt{\mathbf{w}^T\boldsymbol{\Sigma}\mathbf{w}}} \\ \text{subject to:} \quad & \mathbf{1}^T\mathbf{w} = 1 \quad \text{(budget)} \\ & \text{VaR}_{\max} - \mathbf{v}^T\mathbf{w} \geq 0 \quad \text{(VaR limit)} \\ & w_i \leq w_{\max} \quad \forall i \quad \text{(concentration)} \\ & w_i \geq 0 \quad \forall i \quad \text{(long-only)} \end{align}$$
